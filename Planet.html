Chekhov is Alive

We took one of the greatest writers of 19th century and brought his work to life with the technology of the 21st.

AGENCY + CLIENT

SLAVA & Google Creative Lab

RESN ROLE

Design, , Illustration, Animation, Development

PLATFORMS

Desktop, Tablet, Mobile

LAUNCH SITE
PLAY

Which Character Are You?

Chekhov Is Alive is an online character quiz and audition created for a marathon livestreamed reading event of Anton Chekhov's plays. The website featured animated illustrations of 28 of Chekhov’s classic characters, wrapped in a playful material design.

A Livestreamed Reading

After taking the quiz, users were able to record and submit an audition to become part of the 24 hour livestreamed reading of Chekhov. During the reading, 700 people performed Chekov on 25 stages, from New York to Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk.

Unprecedented Response

The unprecedented media attention and public interest resulted in massive site traffic and millions of viewers for the livestreaming event. The project breathed life into Chekhov's works and brought him a new generation of fans.

Results

We had 1 million+ site visits, 528,990 took the online test and 850 recorded a video audition. There were 5.4 billion media impressions, 6 million views of promo video, and 3 million playbacks of the livestream. The Google+ page got 20,000 followers and 4 million impressions, while Google searches for Chekhov increased fourfold, after project was announced.

KFC Hall of Colonels

Welcome to the Hall of Colonels, a journey into Colonel Sanders' legendary past.

AGENCY + CLIENT

Wieden+Kennedy Portland & KFC

RESN ROLE

Design, Animation, Development

PLATFORMS

Desktop, Tablet, Mobile

LAUNCH SITE
PLAY

History's Greatest Chicken Chef

In this virtual animatronic museum, you’ll find games, music, theatre and a whole lot more. From fistfights to shootouts, you’ll learn facts about the Colonel’s life you won’t believe. It’s like a boring history lesson without the boring.

Real Fake

In a fit of hubris, we decided to make physical objects. Yes, we made non-digital things! Things you can feel. Or rub up against. Or poke with a stick. We know, it's weird. With the help of some special effects make-up artists, we recreated the head of Colonel Sanders three times: one young, one old and one bronze. The last one was to represent the deceased Colonel (because somehow it seemed too soon for a zombie version). Colonel Sanders might not have lived to see the digital age, but a phalanx of his prosthetic lookalikes is the next best thing.

Colonel Quest

The taste of KFC reminds us of a simpler time. Like the Great Depression, for instance (which, apparently, wasn't all that great), or the 1980s (which totally was). In our case, it was the era of classic console games. Which is why we made Colonel Quest - an epic cycle of six 8-bit games based on true events from the Colonel's life. Forget your over-engineered modern controllers, these games are played with just two buttons. They’re finger clickin' good.

Drumstick Gaming

To promote the site, we made game controllers out of real imitation chicken drumsticks and sent them to high profile influencers. Most of them were just confused, but some people thought they were awesome. And the second sort of people were right.

The Self-Destructing Book

James Patterson is the world’s #1 bestselling author. His latest book, 'Private Vegas', is about to self-destruct.

AGENCY + CLIENT

Mother New York & James Patterson

RESN ROLE

Design, Animation, Development

PLATFORMS

Desktop, Tablet, Mobile

LAUNCH SITE
PLAY

The Most Thrilling Read Ever

For the launch of James Patterson's 'Private Vegas', Resn and Mother New York wanted to make sure the experience was as exhilarating as possible. To do this, we created the Self-Destructing Book, an electrifying, digitally augmented thrill-ride, befitting the Master of Suspense.

Design & Experience

We developed an iPad-exclusive version of the book, complete with animated thrills at key moments in the action. Through our website, we gave away 1,000 copies to lucky winners, who had 24 hours to read the book before it self-destructed. To ensure only the winners could access it, we created a unique code for every copy and released them in batches over five days.

Dramatic Tension

We designed every aspect of our app to heighten the tension of the reading experience. As the story unfolds, blood splatters across the screen. Smoke obscures the text. Hidden messages appear. Police sirens wail. Explosions envelop the page. In every possible way, we put readers directly into the action.

It's a Sabotage!

After entering the access code, the countdown begins. As a constant reminder, a timer counts down every second, until the very last. To connect site visitors to the reading experience, we let them steal time from our winners. Users were connected in real-time to our readers via the hub. By clicking the 'Sabotage Timer' button, they could steal fifteen minutes of reading time, heightening the tension and forcing winners to read faster.

Play Your Stripes

Resn and Wieden+Kennedy New York partnered to create Gap: Play Your Stripes, an augmented reality experience that turns your winter wardrobe into a musical masterpiece.

AGENCY + CLIENT

Wieden+Kennedy New York & GAP

RESN ROLE

Design, Animation, Development

PLATFORMS

Desktop, Tablet, Mobile

PLAY

Musical Clothing

Our challenge was to let users put on an ordinary striped outfit, stand in front of a webcam, and start making music. It had to be simple, intuitive, and fun. Part of Gap’s Holiday Gift Guide, Play Your Stripes features the song ‘It Is What It Is’ and tracks by Disasteradio.

Rapid Problem Solving

A technology discovery phase allowed us to quickly experiment with different approaches to solve a complex creative problem. To find the best approach, we developed a range of prototypes exploring stripe detection, hand tracking, sample triggering, beat quantizing and audio mixing.

Detecting Stripes and Hands

Our proprietary Smart Stripe Detection System™ analyses colour changes within the image to locate the boundaries of stripes and define the areas that the user can play. To detect hands, we analyse differences in lightness values on the webcam feed. Frame-to-frame, we track user movement and use blob detection to determine the position of the user's hands and detect which stripes they are touching.

Mobile Version

We created a dedicated mobile experience, using CSS animations and HTML audio, because we wanted you to be able to play along with Blood Orange no matter what device you have.

Audio Mixing

Music is dynamically mixed, in real-time. This ensures the audio tracks are in sync with each other and the video. Recorded audio is spliced with the user’s video so they that they can share it on their social networks.

Way of the Desperados

Way of the Desperados. With GIFs from pixel wizards near and far. So drop the sanity act. Put your face in it. And let your pupils party.

AGENCY + CLIENT

Wieden+Kennedy Amsterdam & Heineken

RESN ROLE

Design, Illustration, Animation, Development

PLATFORMS

Desktop, Tablet, Mobile

LAUNCH SITE
PLAY

GIFtastic

Way of the Desperados is an interactive Tumblr experience like no other. The unexpected and surprising GIFs are paired with random rollovers of variable speed, rotation, and scale.

A Fluid Experience

Our top priority was silky-smooth UX. As you scroll through the page, textures are uploaded and removed. To increase the performance, we used the GPU to accelerate the native scroll and to move and animate elements on the page.

DRAG

Structured Creativity

We used the Tumblr API to create a flexible front end. We mirror the page structure to maintain a cohesion between the experience and the base API, giving us creative freedom within the Tumblr platform.

High Performance

Our biggest challenge was enabling users to scroll through an unlimited number of GIFs without compromising performance. The solution was to use a static image when the page is scrolling, then replace it with a GIF when it stops.
